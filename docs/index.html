The United States has had 576,774 confirmed cases.
That's about 2 out of every 1,000 people who we know are or have been infected.

That is 24,948 more cases than yesterday, or a one-day increase of 4.5%. Yesterday there were 28,983 new cases, a one-day increase of 5.5%. The rate of new cases today is lower than yesterday.

The number of confirmed cases is recently growing at about 5.9% per day. That is doubling about every 12.1 days. At the current rate, the total number of confirmed cases will be about 860,000 in 7 days.

The following chart shows the rate-of-change over time of different statistics. The blue line shows the rate of change in the total confirmed infections. The orange line shows the rate of change in the total number of tests administered. The black line shows the rate of change in the total number of deaths.

South Dakota has the fastest-growing rate of new infections at about 16.8% per day. That is doubling about every 4.5 days. South Dakota currently has 730 confirmed cases, but at the current rate, it will have about 2,160 in 7 days. Rhode Island has the second fastest-growing rate of new infections at about 16.5% per day.
