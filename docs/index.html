The United States has had 332,308 confirmed cases.
That's about 1 out of every 1,000 people who we know are or have been infected.

The number of confirmed cases is recently growing at about 12.1% per day. That is doubling about every 6.1 days. At the current rate, the total number of confirmed cases will be about 737,739 in 7 days.

The following chart shows the rate-of-change over time of different statistics. The blue line shows the rate of change in the total confirmed infections. The orange line shows the rate of change in the total number of tests administered. The black line shows the rate of change in the total number of deaths.

Idaho has the fastest-growing rate of new infections at about 20.2% per day. That is doubling about every 3.8 days. Idaho currently has 1,077 confirmed cases, but at the current rate, it will have about 3,894 in 7 days. Louisiana has the second fastest-growing rate of new infections at about 20.1% per day. That's doubling about every 3.8 days, and will cause total cases to increase from 13,010 to 46,917 in 7 days.
