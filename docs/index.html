The United States has had 492,678 confirmed cases.
That's about 1 out of every 1,000 people who we know are or have been infected.

That is 34,204 more cases than yesterday, or a one-day increase of 7.5%. Yesterday there were 34,083 new cases, a one-day increase of 8.0%. The rate of new cases today is a little lower than yesterday.

The number of confirmed cases is recently growing at about 7.9% per day. That is doubling about every 9.1 days. At the current rate, the total number of confirmed cases will be about 840,000 in 7 days.

The following chart shows the rate-of-change over time of different statistics. The blue line shows the rate of change in the total confirmed infections. The orange line shows the rate of change in the total number of tests administered. The black line shows the rate of change in the total number of deaths.

Delaware has the fastest-growing rate of new infections at about 19.5% per day. That is doubling about every 3.9 days. Delaware currently has 1,326 confirmed cases, but at the current rate, it will have about 4,602 in 7 days. South Dakota has the second fastest-growing rate of new infections at about 16.9% per day. That's doubling about every 4.4 days, and will cause total cases to increase from 536 to 1,598 in 7 days.
